Essay Structure Guide

Paragraph 1 — Introduction + Thesis

●  Open with a general statement that restates the theme in your own words
●  Broaden the context with 1–2 sentences of background/relevance
●  End with a thesis sentence that previews all 3 bullet points without listing them

mechanically

Pattern:

[General observation about the topic] + [why it matters today] + [thesis
previewing your 3 points]

Paragraph 2 — Body (all 3 points combined)

●  Address each bullet point in the same order as the prompt
●  Each point gets 2–3 sentences: one to introduce it, one or two to develop/explain
●  Use linking words to connect points smoothly rather than starting each with "Firstly /

Secondly / Thirdly"

Recommended connectors:

Transition

Use for

On one hand / On the other
hand

pros vs cons

adding a point

parallel ideas

Paragraph 3 — Conclusion

●  Never introduce new ideas
●  Open with In conclusion or Ultimately
●  Restate the thesis in different words
●  End with a broad, forward-looking statement about implications or what is at stake

Common mistakes to avoid:

●  Do not use "I think" or "I believe" — use it appears, one might argue, it is clear that
●  Do not bullet point inside the essay
●  Do not repeat the exact wording of the prompt in your introduction
●  Do not start the conclusion with the same word as the introduction
